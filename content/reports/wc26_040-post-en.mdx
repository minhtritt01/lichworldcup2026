# New Zealand 1–3 Egypt: Salah Shines as Egypt Seizes Group G Control

## Match Narrative

New Zealand opened with encouraging intensity, pressing Egypt's defense and creating early chances through energetic movement in midfield. The All Whites' compact shape frustrated Egypt's build-up play for the opening twenty minutes, with Tommy Smith and Ryan Thomas snapping at opposition heels. However, Egypt's quality told as the half progressed. Mohamed Salah broke the deadlock in the 34th minute with a clinical finish after Tarek Hamed's incisive through ball caught the All Whites flat-footed. Trezeguet doubled the lead just before halftime with a close-range tap-in following confused defending at a corner. New Zealand emerged revitalized after the break, with substitute Marko Stamenic pulling one back in the 62nd minute to set up a tense final stage. But Egypt's composure proved decisive when Akram Tawfik's driving run and cross allowed Salah to seal victory with his second goal, deflating New Zealand's comeback hopes and underscoring Egypt's midfield and attacking superiority.

## Tactical Breakdown

New Zealand deployed a disciplined 4-2-3-1, with Finn Surman and Elijah Just protecting the backline and focusing on compact, low-block defending to limit Egypt's creative space. Manager Phil Ferns' strategy hinged on quick transitions and set-piece threat. Egypt, meanwhile, operated in a fluid 4-3-3 with Hamed orchestrating play from deep. Ashraf's positioning on the left wing allowed inverted passing angles, while Salah and Trezeguet operated as roaming threats. Egypt's superiority emerged in possession retention and press resistance—Hamed and Omar Kamal recycled play effortlessly, gradually suffocating New Zealand's midfield energy. After conceding in the 62nd minute, Egypt shifted slightly more defensive, playing 4-4-2 to protect the backline while still using Salah's running to exploit wide spaces. Tawfik's tactical evolution into an attacking outlet in the second half exploited New Zealand's fatigue, turning what could've been a nervy finish into Egypt's controlled victory.
