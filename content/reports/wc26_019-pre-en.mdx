## Tactical Analysis

Argentina will likely deploy a 4-3-3 formation, leveraging their width through Tagliafico and Montiel while Paredes and De Paul control the midfield tempo. The key battle will be in the center of the park—Algeria's Belaïd and Zerrouki must disrupt Argentina's possession dominance to prevent Messi from operating in dangerous spaces. Algeria's main weakness is defensive vulnerability to quick transitions; De Paul's energy and Álvarez's movement could exploit gaps behind their fullbacks. Argentina's aerial weakness, evident from the Iceland match, presents Algeria's best avenue for danger. Mandi and Tougai must capitalize on set pieces. The midfield duel between Mahrez's creativity and Argentina's pressing will determine whether Algeria transitions smoothly to attacks. Ultimately, Argentina's technical superiority should control possession, but Algeria's compact 4-2-3-1 shape could frustrate and create counter-attacking opportunities.
