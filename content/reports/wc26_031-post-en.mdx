## Match Narrative

Brazil's clinical opening assault quickly overwhelmed Haiti, who found themselves pinned back from the opening whistle at Lincoln Financial Field. The Seleção's attacking trident of Vinícius Júnior, Neymar, and Raphinha combined seamlessly to probe Haiti's shallow defensive lines. Raphinha broke the deadlock in the 23rd minute with a clinical finish following Bruno Guimarães' pinpoint pass through the midfield. Haiti's resistance crumbled thereafter. A second-half implosion saw Matheus Cunha double the lead in the 57th minute after exploiting space left by an ambitious Haitian push forward. Neymar sealed the outcome in the 78th minute, curling a trademark effort beyond Johny Placide to secure an emphatic victory. Brazil's dominance never wavered, their midfield control rendering Haiti's limited counter-attacking threats entirely irrelevant throughout the 90 minutes.

## Tactical Breakdown

Brazil's 4-2-3-1 setup with Casemiro and Bruno Guimarães as a double pivot enabled fluid transitions between defense and attack. Vinícius Júnior's intensity on the left flank overwhelmed Haiti's defensive structure, frequently forcing Alex Sandro into attacking positions. Haiti deployed a predictable 5-4-1 in defensive formation, with Carlens Arcus and Ricardo Adé forming a stoic but porous defensive backbone. Five defenders could not contain Brazil's positional play—Raphinha drifted centrally to create numerical superiority in midfield, while Neymar's free-roaming role created constant defensive headaches. Haiti's narrow, congested shape left the flanks dangerously vulnerable; Brazil's fullbacks exploited this ruthlessly. By the 60th minute, Brazil's tactical superiority was absolute, and they managed the game with minimal resistance from their overwhelmed opponents.

## Key Moments

The pivotal juncture arrived in the 57th minute when Matheus Cunha intercepted a misplaced pass from Josué Duverger just inside Haiti's half. Cunha's explosive burst down the left channel, accelerating past both covering defenders, created the separation needed for a low finish past Placide's outstretched hand. This second goal transformed tactical desperation into certainty—Haiti's defensive structure, already fragmentary under Brazil's intensity, finally fractured completely. The goal exemplified Brazil's patient, precise buildup play converting limited space into decisive opportunities. Neymar's 78th-minute strike, arriving after Vinícius Júnior's cutback from the byline, merely confirmed the inevitable and put the result beyond mathematical dispute.

## Aftermath

Brazil's commanding 3-0 victory positions them as Group C favorites heading into subsequent matches. Manager Dorival Júnior's tactical setup—aggressive pressing, inverted winger positioning, and Bruno Guimarães' ball-progression brilliance—established a blueprint for future knockout-round encounters. Haiti, despite spirited early attempts to maintain shape, faces an uphill climb to accumulate meaningful points in this group. The Caribbean side's limited technical capacity and uneven defensive organization proved insufficient against Brazil's multifaceted attacking scheme. Goal differential now heavily favors the Seleção, whose commanding advantage provides significant buffer over the tournament's closing group matches. Haiti must regroup and target points from other opponents, though this opening demonstration of Brazil's depth and destructive capability will weigh psychologically on all Group C rivals.
