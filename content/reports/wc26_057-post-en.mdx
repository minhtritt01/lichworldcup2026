# Japan 1–1 Sweden: AT&T Stadium

Japan and Sweden served up a pulsating draw that rewarded neither side despite contrasting approaches to Group F's most tactically intriguing fixture. Sweden struck first in the 28th minute through a sharp counterattack, Emil Krafth's overlapping run creating space for a cut-back finish that caught Japan's zonal marking napping. Japan's response was methodical—waves of possession-based attacks probing for the underexposed Swedish flanks—but Robin Olsen stood firm through the first half. The decisive moment arrived in the 67th minute when Wataru Endo's driving run drew a rash challenge in the penalty area. Hidemasa Morita converted coolly, sending Olsen the wrong way to level the contest. Both teams created further chances but neither could find a second breakthrough, leaving both sides ruefully contemplating missed qualification opportunities.

Sweden countered with a compact 4-4-2, emphasizing defensive discipline and transition efficiency. Japan's introduction of more direct passing in the 55th minute forced Sweden deeper; in response, coach adjusted to a 5-3-2 to absorb pressure. The match became a battle between Japan's technical creativity and Sweden's structural resilience—ultimately neither tactic proved decisive.
