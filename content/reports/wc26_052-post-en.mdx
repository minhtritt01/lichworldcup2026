## Match Narrative

Morocco dominated possession and territory from kickoff, with Haiti content to absorb pressure and exploit counter-attacking opportunities. The Atlas Lions controlled the first half rhythm, creating consistent chances through their fluid midfield. Haiti's compact defensive shape frustrated Morocco early, but the breakthrough came in the 23rd minute when a well-timed run down the left flank resulted in the opening goal. Morocco's second followed swiftly before halftime, seemingly putting the contest beyond doubt. However, Haiti emerged with renewed intent after the break, scoring twice within ten minutes to level at 2-2 and spark genuine belief. The momentum shift startled Morocco momentarily, but their quality and experience reasserted itself. Two clinical finishes in the final twenty minutes—one from open play, another from a set piece—sealed a commanding 4-2 victory and Group C leadership.

## Tactical Breakdown

Morocco deployed a 4-3-3 formation, prioritizing width through Hakimi and Mazraoui while maintaining a disciplined central midfield four. Haiti countered in a compact 4-4-2 block, pressing sporadically and relying on direct play to transition quickly. Morocco's ball circulation was crisp and patient, stretching Haiti laterally to create overloads on the flanks. Yassine Bounou's distribution from the back proved instrumental in breaking Haiti's mid-block. Haiti's tactical adjustment at halftime—pushing higher up the pitch to compress space—initially paid dividends, catching Morocco's back line caught out of position twice. However, this left them vulnerable to the counter-attack. Morocco's second-half substitutions added fresh legs and directness to the midfield, allowing them to regain control. The decisive tactical shift came when Morocco dropped deeper defensively while maintaining possession superiority, neutralizing Haiti's aerial threat and negating their long-ball strategy entirely.

## Key Moments

The pivotal moment arrived at 62 minutes. Haiti had just equalized at 2-2 following a scrappy goalmouth scramble, and momentum had swung decisively toward the underdogs. Morocco appeared rattled, their rhythm disrupted. The turning point came when a Morocco midfielder intercepted a loose Haiti pass in midfield and launched a devastating counter-attack. Haiti's resistance crumbled thereafter, their energy and organization disintegrating. The 4-2 victory propels Morocco to the summit of Group C with maximum points, demonstrating their credentials as contenders despite a scare in the second half. Haiti's spirited comeback showcased their fighting spirit but also exposed their defensive vulnerabilities against higher-quality opposition; two goals down, they briefly inspired belief, yet ultimately lacked the tactical discipline to maintain their resurgence. Morocco's manager will acknowledge Haiti's second-half improvement as a cautionary tale—even favorites face danger when complacency sets in—though the clinical finishing and experience to reestablish control will encourage confidence moving forward.
