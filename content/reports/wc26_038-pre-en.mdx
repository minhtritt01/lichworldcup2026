# Belgium vs Iran — Group G Pre-Match Analysis

## Tactical Preview

Belgium under Domenico Tedesco will field their most experienced available lineup, centering the system around Kevin De Bruyne's orchestration from central midfield. Axel Witsel's defensive cover as a single pivot allows De Bruyne and Youri Tielemans to operate with greater freedom, while Romelu Lukaku's physical presence up front provides the aerial target and hold-up play that creates space for Jeremy Doku's direct, explosive runs from the left. Iran under Carlos Queiroz will deploy a disciplined 4-3-3 that compresses into a 4-5-1 when defending, Saeid Ezatolahi providing the defensive midfield anchor and Alireza Jahanbakhsh being released to press high. Iran's primary attacking strategy will be direct — utilizing Mehdi Taremi's technical excellence and physical power as a lone striker capable of bringing teammates into play. Sardar Azmoun's intelligent movement from wide positions creates the combinations Iran need to produce attacking moments of quality.

## Form & Momentum

Belgium's "golden generation" era may be passing its peak, but De Bruyne, Courtois, Lukaku and Doku remain a formidable attacking combination at tournament level. The emergence of younger players like Jeremy Doku provides the dynamism that the next generation brings, while the experience accumulated through the squad's senior core ensures Belgium can manage games of this importance. Iran's World Cup history includes shock results — their 2022 campaign demonstrated that Queiroz can organize defensive structures capable of frustrating far more technically gifted opponents. Taremi's move to Inter Milan has elevated his technical development significantly, and his ability to link play under pressure from deep positions may provide Iran with more sustained attacking periods than their FIFA ranking suggests is achievable.

For Iran, Mehdi Taremi's ability to combine physically with Azmoun and draw defenders into undesirable positions creates the window for Iran's most threatening attacking moments.
