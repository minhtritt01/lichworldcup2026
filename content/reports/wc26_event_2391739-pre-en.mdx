## Tactical Analysis

Spain's recent draw with Iraq signals defensive vulnerabilities that Cape Verde may attempt to exploit through direct play. La Roja typically employ a possession-dominated 4-3-3, with Rodri orchestrating from midfield, but they'll likely face a compact, counter-attacking setup from Cape Verde. The battle zone will be Spain's flanks—where Dani Carvajal and Alejandro Balde must contend with Cape Verde's wing play. Cape Verde, likely deploying a 4-4-2, will prioritize defensive organization and set pieces, as their strength lies in physical presence rather than technical superiority. Spain's midfield control should dominate possession (65%+), but they must avoid complacency and careless turnovers. Cape Verde's primary threat comes through quick transitions; limiting Garry Rodrigues and Jamiro Monteiro's space will be critical for Spain's success.

## Form & Momentum

Spain's draw with Iraq is a red flag ahead of their World Cup campaign—a slip-up against a competitive but not elite opponent suggests tactical issues or mental sharpness concerns. However, they remain heavy favorites given their pedigree. Cape Verde's victory over Bermuda demonstrates resilience and hunger, providing genuine confidence entering this match. Psychologically, Spain faces pressure to deliver a commanding statement after a stuttering start, while Cape Verde arrives as underdogs with nothing to lose, potentially allowing them to play with freedom. The narrative advantage slightly favors Spain on paper, but their inability to dispatch Iraq confidently has tempered expectations. Cape Verde's David vs. Goliath scenario may energize them—Group H's "easier" opponent often harbors dangerous ambition.

## Predicted Lineup & Key Players

Spain will field an attacking unit built around Rodri's midfield mastery, with Pedri operating from the left-wing slot. David Raya or Unai Simón will start in goal, while the back four features Carvajal, Robin Le Normand, Aymeric Laporte, and Balde—a blend of experience and youth. Cape Verde's lineup centers on Jamiro Monteiro and Garry Rodrigues as attacking outlets; Stopira and Logan Costa provide defensive ballast. Rodri will be Spain's standout performer, dictating tempo and breaking Cape Verde's lines through precise distribution. For Cape Verde, Garry Rodrigues—their most creative player—poses the greatest threat on the counter. Watchlists also include Pedri's creative contributions for Spain and Jovane Cabral's physical directness for Cape Verde.
