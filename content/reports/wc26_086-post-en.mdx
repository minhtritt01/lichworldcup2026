# Argentina 3–2 Cape Verde — Hard Rock Stadium

## Match Narrative

Argentina's Group Stage opener at Hard Rock Stadium delivered exactly the kind of drama that defines World Cup football: an underdogged Cape Verde side arrived refusing to be mere spectators, mounting a second-half assault that nearly upended Scaloni's defending champions. Argentina dominated possession in the first half through familiar passing patterns, with De Paul and Paredes controlling the tempo from midfield. Messi's presence orchestrated movement, and Álvarez's pressing created the space for early chances. Cape Verde absorbed pressure intelligently, then struck back through clinical finishing that exposed Argentina's transitional vulnerabilities. The African side's pressing game in the second half forced turnovers in dangerous areas, leading to their two strikes. But Argentina's experience and technical quality proved decisive in the final moments—substitutions brought fresh legs, and a moment of composure in the 82nd minute sealed the contest.

## Tactical Breakdown

Argentina operated in a familiar 4-3-3, with Montiel and Tagliafico stretched on the flanks providing width while Martínez and Balerdi formed a reliable defensive core. De Paul and Paredes maintained midfield control, allowing Messi freedom to drift into dangerous pockets. Cape Verde countered with a compact 4-4-2 block, prioritizing vertical passes and wing play to exploit Argentina's high fullbacks. Their midfield—led by Jovane Cabral and Jamiro Monteiro—pressed aggressively in transition, trying to disrupt Argentina's build-up play. In the second half, Cape Verde shifted to a more aggressive 4-2-3-1, pushing forward when they sensed Argentina's rhythm faltering. Scaloni responded by introducing fresh attacking options, which overwhelmed Cape Verde's tiring back line and restored Argentina's control, ultimately forcing defensive mistakes that decided the match.

## Key Moments

The turning point arrived in the 72nd minute when Álvarez surged into the box following a Messi through-ball that split Cape Verde's midfield. His low finish across Vozinha gave Argentina the 3-1 lead, seemingly ending the contest. However, Cape Verde's resilience was underscored when Garry Rodrigues converted a penalty in the 78th minute after Balerdi's handball, setting up a tense finale. Cape Verde pressed desperately for an equalizer, their attacking intent genuine and dangerous, but Argentina's experience in such moments proved crucial. A final clearance and midfield control in stoppage time preserved the lead, with Messi's presence offering calm distribution that allowed Argentina to run down the clock.

## Aftermath

Argentina's 3-2 victory places them atop their group with three points, though the narrow margin will prompt tactical questions for Scaloni ahead of knockout stages. Cape Verde's defeat comes with moral credit—a well-organized team that demonstrated technical competence and fighting spirit, threatening the continental favorites. The scoreline reflects Argentina's championship mentality but also highlights vulnerabilities in defending transitions that superior opponents may exploit. Scaloni acknowledged Cape Verde's intensity in post-match remarks, hinting at midfield reinforcements for the next fixture. For Cape Verde, the performance offers a blueprint for future campaigns and proves African representation remains competitive at football's highest level.
