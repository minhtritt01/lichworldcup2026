The Netherlands dominated possession from the opening whistle, controlling 63% of play and dictating tempo through Frenkie de Jong and Reijnders in midfield. Morocco, however, refused to wilt. ## Tactical Breakdown

Louis van Gaal's Netherlands operated a fluid 4-2-3-1, with van Dijk and Timber forming a composed center-back pairing while Dumfries pushed high on the right. The midfield duo of de Jong and Reijnders orchestrated build-up play, with Xavi Simons and Gakpo interchanging on the wings. Morocco countered with a 4-4-2 block—disciplined, compact, and designed to deny space in transition.

Post-equalizer, Morocco withdrew further, inviting Dutch dominance but accepting territorial loss for defensive security—a pragmatic adjustment that paid dividends.

The 34th minute saw the Netherlands break the deadlock. De Jong collected possession midway, played a one-two with Gakpo, and slipped a pass into Depay, who arrived at the penalty spot unmarked and buried it clinically—a trademark Oranje sequence.

Morocco's response arrived in the 67th minute. A misplaced Netherlands clearance found Belammari on the left flank. His low cross evaded van Dijk's feet, and Belammari himself finished from eight yards with precision. Both sides created chances thereafter—Simons struck wide in the 78th minute, and Zakaria El Ouahdi forced Verbruggen into a fine save—but the scoreline would not budge.

Penalties proved merciless. Morocco's nerve held: Hakimi, Belammari, and El Ouahdi converted; the Netherlands converted only Reijnders and Gakpo before Dumfries' crucial miss sealed it. Bounou's save on the crucial spot-kick proved the difference.
