# Tunisia vs Japan — Group F Pre-Match Analysis

## Tactical Preview

Tunisia's Jalel Kadri will organize his side in a 4-3-3, relying on Hannibal Mejbri's energy and Ellyes Skhiri's physicality to control the midfield zone. Wahbi Khazri's experience and creativity from the left will be Tunisia's primary attacking outlet, while Youssef Msakni's movement as the central striker provides unpredictability. Japan under Hajime Moriyasu will press with the intensity and coherence that has become their tournament trademark, Wataru Endo providing the defensive solidity that frees Kamada and Morita to advance aggressively. Japan's attacking trio of Minamino, Osako, and Reo Hatate is technically precise and direct, capable of exploiting the spaces that Tunisia's aggressive wide players leave when they commit forward. The fundamental tactical contest will be: can Tunisia's midfield three handle Japan's pressing and establish enough possession to create chances, or will Japan's structured high press force errors and generate the volume of attacks their superior tactical organization should produce?

## Form & Momentum

Tunisia represent the African tradition of technical, compact football combined with individual creativity in wide positions. Their AFCON campaign and World Cup qualification demonstrated resilience and tactical adaptability. Hannibal Mejbri's continued development at Manchester United adds a European dimension to their approach, and Khazri's veteran presence provides crucial experience at tournament level. Japan enter this match having qualified from the most competitive AFC region in recent tournament cycles, having developed tactical sophistication and pressing intensity that has made them consistently dangerous at World Cups. Their technical foundation, combined with the emergence of talented attackers like Hatate from Celtic and Minamino, gives Japan a balanced squad that can genuinely trouble any opponent willing to let them dictate tempo.

**Tunisia (4-3-3):** Aymen Dahmen; Montassar Talbi, Nader Ghandri, Bilel Ifa, Mohamed Dräger; Hannibal Mejbri, Aïssa Laïdouni, Ellyes Skhiri; Naïm Sliti, Youssef Msakni, Wahbi Khazri

**Japan (4-3-3):** Shuichi Gonda; Hiroki Sakai, Yuto Nagatomo, Ko Itakura, Shogo Taniguchi; Wataru Endo, Hidemasa Morita, Daichi Kamada; Takumi Minamino, Yuya Osako, Reo Hatate

**Players to Watch:** Wataru Endo's reading of the game and disruption of midfield passing lanes is fundamental to Japan's success; his ability to win second balls and launch quick transitions gives Japan's attacking players the platform they need. For Tunisia, Ellyes Skhiri's defensive midfield work must match Japan's physicality and technical intensity or the entire Tunisian structure will be overrun.

## Score Prediction & Match Odds

Japan's cohesion, pressing intensity and technical quality give them the edge against a Tunisia side whose individual creativity is less reliable than Japan's collective system. Expect Japan to control the tempo, force errors, and convert through their technically excellent attacking unit. **Prediction: Tunisia 0-3 Japan.** A commanding Japanese victory underlining their status as one of Asia's finest World Cup representatives. **Match Odds:** Tunisia 3.20, Draw 3.30, Japan 2.30.
