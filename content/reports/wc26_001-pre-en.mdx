## Tactical Analysis

Mexico will likely employ their traditional 4-3-3 formation, leveraging their possession-based approach and width through Jesús Gallardo and Jorge Sánchez. The midfield triangle of Edson Álvarez, Orbelín Pineda, and one attacking midfielder will control tempo, while their defensive structure relies on compactness. South Africa typically counter in a 4-2-3-1 setup, prioritizing defensive solidity and exploiting transition moments. Mexico's key battle zone is the right flank—Gallardo's attacking runs can overwhelm South Africa's defensive organization if Khuliso Mudau isn't protected adequately. Conversely, South Africa's weakness is midfield creativity; they lack a dominant playmaker to break Mexico's press. Mexico should dominate possession (55-60%), but South Africa's physicality and set-piece threat—particularly from Modiba and Sibisi—cannot be underestimated in the box.

South Africa needs a positive result to validate their tournament credentials.
