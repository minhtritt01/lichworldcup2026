## Tactical Analysis

Spain will likely deploy their traditional 4-3-3 formation, leveraging possession-based football and control through midfield. With Rodri and Pedri orchestrating play, Spain creates numerical superiority in dangerous zones and exploits flanks through fullback progression. Cape Verde, expected to line up in a 4-4-2, will employ a compact defensive shape focused on compressing space and limiting midfield dominance. The critical battle zone will be Spain's left flank, where Balde faces direct pressure from Cape Verde's wing play. Spain's weakness lies in early-match intensity—their draw with Iraq suggests vulnerability to direct, high-pressing opponents. Cape Verde's defensive discipline will be tested repeatedly, but their lack of technical depth in midfield creates opportunities for Spain's press to force turnovers. Expect Spain to dominate possession (65-70%) while seeking to break down a resolute but limited defensive structure.

## Form & Momentum

Spain's draw against Iraq is concerning—a setback for a team of their caliber—indicating inconsistent rhythm and potential complacency. The result suggests they failed to impose their typical dominance, highlighting concentration issues that must be addressed. Conversely, Cape Verde's victory over Bermuda demonstrates fighting spirit and defensive solidity, but the opposition quality difference is stark. Spain possess superior tournament experience, technical cohesion, and historical pedigree, factors that typically translate to increased confidence. However, Cape Verde's underdog status and recent positive result provide psychological advantage through low expectations and unity. Spain will be favored to respond decisively, using the Iraq disappointment as motivation to demonstrate their World Cup credentials against a lesser opponent.
