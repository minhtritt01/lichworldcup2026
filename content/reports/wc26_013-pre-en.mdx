## Tactical Analysis

Spain will deploy their characteristic 4-3-3 formation, leveraging possession dominance and positional fluidity. Rodri's deep playmaking from midfield will orchestrate transitions, while wing-backs Carvajal and Balde provide width for combinations with inside forwards Pedri and Gavi. Cape Verde will likely counter with a compact 4-4-2 or 5-4-1, prioritizing defensive solidity and compact spacing to minimize Spain's passing lanes. The critical battle zone will be Spain's midfield third—if Cape Verde can pressure Rodri aggressively and block central passing corridors, they disrupt Spain's rhythm. Spain's primary weakness lies in their defensive transitions; Cape Verde's quick counter opportunities via Jovane Cabral could exploit spaces when Spain overcommits forward. Set pieces represent Cape Verde's best scoring chance against a Spanish side that occasionally looks vulnerable at defending standardized routines.

## Form & Momentum

Spain's draw against Iraq is a significant concern heading into this fixture. The result suggests neither the intensity nor clinical finishing expected from a World Cup favorite, potentially indicating fatigue, tactical inflexibility, or complacency. Cape Verde's victory over Bermuda provides genuine confidence and momentum, though the quality gap between opponents remains considerable. Psychologically, Spain will feel pressure to respond with dominance; Cape Verde enters without expectation, enabling a relaxed approach and potential to frustrate. However, Spain's tournament pedigree and experience typically reassert themselves after unconvincing performances. The narrative of Spain "getting serious" post-Iraq carries psychological weight—they know early World Cup stumbles demand immediate correction.
