## Match Narrative

Croatia dominated the opening 25 minutes, controlling possession and methodically probing Ghana's back line. Andrej Kramarić opened the scoring in the 18th minute after Mateo Kovačić released him down the left channel with a perfectly-timed through ball. Ghana responded admirably after half-time, with Thomas Partey orchestrating a brisk counter-attack that resulted in Daniel Kofi Kyereh equalizing in the 58th minute, catching Croatia's defense momentarily off-guard. The decisive moment came in the 72nd minute when Luka Modrić curled a magnificent free-kick from 25 yards that goalkeeper Ati Zigi could only parry weakly, allowing Ante Budimir to tap home the winner from close range. Croatia held firm in the final stages despite Ghana's desperate pressure, managing the game through Kovačić's midfield mastery and experience.

## Tactical Breakdown

Croatia deployed their characteristic 4-3-3 formation, with Modrić and Kovačić controlling the midfield tempo while Kramarić roamed fluidly in the attacking third. Ghana countered with a compact 4-2-3-1, banking on Partey and Iddrisu Baba's disciplined shield in midfield to break up play. Croatia's width came through the aggressive full-backs—Stanišić and Ćaleta-Car pushed high to create numerical overloads on the flanks. Midway through the second half, Ghana's manager adjusted tactically by pushing Tariq Lamptey higher, creating a more aggressive 3-5-2, but this unintentionally opened dangerous lanes for Croatian transitions. Kovačić's press-resistance and distribution quality allowed Croatia to consistently bypass Ghana's midfield press. Despite Ghana's admirable energy and work-rate, they lacked the technical depth to sustain pressure against Croatia's superiority in possession.

## Key Moments

The pivotal moment arrived in the 72nd minute when Modrić won a free-kick 25 yards from goal after Alexander Djiku's cynical foul on the Croatian playmaker. Standing over the ball with Ghana expecting a direct strike down the middle, Modrić bent a sublime right-footed effort that dipped gracefully over the wall and caught Ati Zigi flat-footed on his line. The goalkeeper's weak parry proved immediately fatal—Ante Budimir, hovering opportunistically at the far post, stabbed the rebound past the scrambling defense into the back of the net. This goal proved the decisive psychological turning point: Ghana's hopes of a comeback effectively evaporated in that instant, and Croatia's experience and composure shone through brightly in managing the remaining 20 minutes despite Ghana's sustained intense pressure.

## Aftermath

The hard-fought victory moved Croatia to three valuable points in Group L and positions them firmly in the knockout conversation with a superior goal difference over their nearest rivals in the group. Ghana's defeat doesn't entirely eliminate them from contention but demands immediate tactical improvement and more clinical finishing in their next fixture to stay alive. Croatia's manager Dalic lauded his team's composure and midfield control: "We didn't panic when Ghana equalized. Our experience and quality showed when it mattered most." Ghana's manager acknowledged the quality gap: "We fought hard, but Croatia's experience and technical level proved decisive. We must regroup and improve significantly."
