# France Edges Past Resolute Paraguay in Tight Group Stage Battle

## Match Narrative

France dominated possession but found Paraguay's compact defensive shape frustratingly difficult to breach for most of the first half. The South American side set up in a disciplined 5-3-2, inviting pressure while looking to exploit pace on the counter through Miguel Almirón and Junior Alonso. The breakthrough came in the 67th minute when France's pressing intensity finally cracked Paraguay's backline. A sequences of quick passes through midfield saw Aurélien Tchouaméni find space on the edge of the box, his weighted pass releasing Théo Hernández down the left flank. The fullback's low cross was converted decisively at the far post, though celebrations were muted given the one-goal margin against a team still pressing for an equalizer in the final stages.

## Tactical Breakdown

Paraguay's defensive approach was built to suffocate France's attacking rhythm. Coach Gustavo Alfaro deployed a five-man backline anchored by Fabián Balbuena's experience, with Omar Alderete providing cover alongside him. The midfield trio of Andrés Cubas, Mathías Villasaint, and Rodrigo Muñoz focused on screening rather than creativity. France, conversely, pressed high with a 4-3-3 shape, using N'Golo Kanté's tireless running to win the ball in dangerous areas. Dayot Upamecano and William Saliba controlled the air and ground equally, while Benjamin Pavard's attacking contributions from right-back stretched Paraguay's defensive resources. As the match progressed, France shifted to wider play, bypassing Paraguay's midfield congestion with long balls to their fullbacks.

## Key Moments

The decisive moment arrived in the 67th minute with France working the ball methodically downfield. After a Paraguay clearance fell to Aurélien Tchouaméni just inside the center circle, the midfielder spotted space between the lines. A first-touch pass to Théo Hernández initiated a lightning-quick transition. Hernández burst down the left wing, checking his run to delay his cross and draw Paraguay's right-sided defenders toward him. His subsequent low ball across the six-yard box found an arriving forward at the far post, who made no mistake with a clinical finish. Paraguay pushed forward desperately in the final twenty minutes, but France's midfield control—anchored by Kanté's defensive prowess—proved sufficient to preserve the clean sheet and the three points.

## Aftermath

The victory propels France to the top of their group with three points and an impressive goal difference advantage. Paraguay remains pointless but showed enough defensive structure to suggest they remain competitive in subsequent fixtures. France's manager will be satisfied with the efficiency, though acknowledging that converting dominance into a more comfortable scoreline will be essential against stronger opposition. For Paraguay, this result underscores the challenge of competing without sustained attacking outlets; Almirón's isolated performances highlighted their lack of creative support. Both teams will regroup with clearer tactical pictures ahead of their next group assignments, France confident in their superiority, Paraguay searching for tactical adjustments that provide greater attacking balance without compromising defensive solidity.
