## Match Narrative

France edged past a tenacious Senegal side 2-1 at MetLife Stadium in a gripping Group I opener that required significant concentration to see out. Les Bleus controlled the early exchanges with their characteristic fluidity, Kylian Mbappé pulling wide to exploit Jules Koundé's overlapping runs while Ousmane Dembélé probed from the opposite flank. The deadlock was broken in the 34th minute when Mbappé cut inside from the right, dropping his shoulder to leave Moussa Niakhaté flat-footed before driving a low left-footed shot beneath Édouard Mendy's outstretched glove. France appeared to have the match under control, their midfield trio of Kanté, Tchouaméni, and Rabiot suffocating Senegal's ability to build from deep. Sadio Mané provided a brief scare when his cleverly flicked through-ball released Nicolas Jackson into space, but William Saliba produced a covering tackle of supreme composure to preserve the lead at half-time.

## Tactical Breakdown

Didier Deschamps fielded a compact 4-3-3 with Kanté anchoring deep and Tchouaméni providing vertical thrust. Mbappé's inverted role from the right gave Senegal's left-sided defenders problems they were ill-equipped to solve. France's press was relentless in the opening forty minutes, denying Idrissa Gueye and Cheikhou Kouyaté the time to distribute. Aliou Cissé's Senegal responded after the interval with a more direct approach, exploiting Mané's physical superiority against Upamecano in aerial duels. When Nicolas Jackson latched onto a Gueye long ball in the 55th minute to equalize with a composed finish across Maignan, the tie suddenly felt open. France responded by throwing on Eduardo Camavinga for the flagging Rabiot, and the Barcelona midfielder's energy immediately changed the tempo. Camavinga forced the decisive moment in the 67th minute with a surging run from deep, carrying the ball thirty yards before slipping a perfectly-weighted pass to Dembélé, whose low cross was turned home by the midfielder himself on a late arrival into the box.

## Key Moments

The 55th-minute equalizer was a thunderbolt for French morale. Jackson, having drifted into space between Koundé and Upamecano, received Gueye's inch-perfect ball over the top and executed a calm side-foot finish with precisely the kind of composure that had justified his inclusion. For a stretch of fifteen minutes, Senegal poured forward with renewed purpose, Mané threatening from deep positions and Ismaïla Sarr stretching the backline. France's response was telling: rather than retreating, Deschamps increased the pressing intensity and the introduction of Camavinga proved masterly. The 67th-minute winner exemplified the tournament pedigree that separates elite nations—France absorbed pressure, made the decisive substitution, and manufactured a winner through dynamic, energetic football that Senegal simply could not cope with.

## Aftermath

France's narrow victory leaves them in control of Group I with three points, though the manner of the win will provide ammunition for critics who feel Les Bleus remain vulnerable to physical, direct opponents. Mbappé's efficiency in front of goal remains unquestionable—his 34th-minute strike was his twelfth goal in major tournament football—and Deschamps' tactical flexibility in utilizing Camavinga from the bench demonstrated the squad depth that makes France genuine contenders. Senegal, despite the defeat, showed enough attacking quality through Mané and Jackson to suggest they remain very much in contention for the knockout rounds. Their pressing structure was occasionally disorganized, and Cissé will need to address the space left in behind the defensive line when they attack in numbers. A response against their next Group I opponents is imperative if they are to advance.
