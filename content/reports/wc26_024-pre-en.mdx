# Uzbekistan vs Colombia — Pre-Match Tactical Analysis

## Tactical Analysis

Uzbekistan will adopt a disciplined 4-2-3-1 formation, prioritizing defensive compactness and counter-attacking efficiency. They drew 0-0 with Venezuela, indicating a well-organized backline and conservative approach. Their strength lies in midfield pressing and quick transitions through Shukurov and Nematov. Colombia, conversely, will deploy a fluid 4-1-4-1, leveraging their technical superiority and width. The battle will be in midfield: Colombia must control possession and prevent Uzbekistan's dangerous counter-attacks, while Uzbekistan needs to disrupt Colombia's rhythm early. Colombia's full-backs (Muñoz, Arias) should exploit Uzbekistan's limited attacking width, but they must remain disciplined defensively. The key vulnerability for Uzbekistan is aerial dominance—Colombia can target set pieces. Expect Colombia to dominate territory but face a stubborn, compact defensive block.

Uzbekistan must avoid an early goal, which would force them away from their defensive structure. The momentum decidedly favors Colombia heading into Estadio Azteca.
