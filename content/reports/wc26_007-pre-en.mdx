## Tactical Analysis

Haiti will likely adopt a compact 4-4-2 or 5-4-1 defensive shape, prioritizing compactness and counter-attacking opportunities given their limited squad depth. Scotland, conversely, will employ their preferred 4-2-3-1 system, leveraging left-back Andy Robertson's attacking prowess down the flank. The critical battleground will be Scotland's right side, where Patterson faces a potential mismatch against Haiti's more limited attacking threats. Haiti's primary vulnerability is defensive transition speed—they conceded heavily to Peru and lack the technical midfield press to disrupt Scottish possession chains. Scotland's weakness lies in creative depth; they rely heavily on set pieces and Robertson's deliveries rather than sustained open-play creation. If Haiti can weather the opening 15 minutes and absorb Scotland's initial pressure, they might exploit long balls to target Scotland's defensive fragility on switches.

Scotland enters with genuine momentum following their convincing victory over Curaçao, demonstrating the collective rhythm and confidence required at tournament level. This psychological edge is substantial—Scotland showed clinical finishing and defensive solidity. Haiti, conversely, suffered a heavy defeat to Peru, likely exposing their defensive inadequacies while simultaneously denting squad morale. The emotional toll of a Group C opener loss cannot be overstated; Haiti faces mounting pressure to produce a result against a confident European side. Scotland's recent form suggests a well-organized unit, while Haiti must rapidly rebuild tactical cohesion and mental resilience. Statistically, teams with back-to-back defeats face compounding psychological pressure. Scotland's victory trajectory provides crucial tournament momentum.
