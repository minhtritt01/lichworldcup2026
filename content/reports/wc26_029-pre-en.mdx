Weston McKennie's box-to-box versatility will be crucial for controlling the midfield tempo. Australia traditionally operates a structured 4-2-3-1, relying on defensive compactness and quick transitions. The key battle zone will be the midfield: if the USA's pressing forces Australia's back line into rushed clearances, they can exploit Aaronson and Pulisic's movement in the attacking third. Conversely, Australia's disciplined shape makes them dangerous on the counter—Leckie's pace and Hrustic's creative distribution could punish American defensive lapses. The USA's central defensive pairing (Richards and Ream) must communicate effectively against Australia's direct approach.
