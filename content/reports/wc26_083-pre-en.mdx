# Colombia vs Croatia — Round of 16 Analysis

## Tactical Analysis

Colombia typically operates in a 4-2-3-1 formation, relying on aggressive pressing and direct transitions. Their strength lies in controlling the midfield with high-energy movement and exploiting spaces on the wings through pace. However, they can be vulnerable to quick counter-attacks, particularly when overcommitting defenders upfield. Croatia, conversely, favors a 4-3-3 structure emphasizing possession and intelligent positioning. They excel at recycling the ball through midfield and building attacks methodically from deep. The key battle zone will be the center of the park—if Colombia dominates this area early, they can suffocate Croatia's rhythm. Conversely, Croatia's technical superiority in possession could expose Colombia's defensive shape on the flanks if they're caught out of position during transitions.

Without detailed recent data, both teams enter the knockout stage with varying confidence levels.
