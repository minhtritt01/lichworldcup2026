## Match Narrative

France controlled proceedings from the opening whistle, establishing midfield dominance through Kanté and Tchouaméni's pressing intensity. Morocco, seeking to exploit wide spaces, initially threatened through Hakimi's overlapping runs but found Pavard and Hernández well-positioned to smother the angles. The first goal arrived in the 34th minute when Rabiot's incisive pass split Morocco's defensive line, allowing Mbappé to finish clinically past Bounou. France's superiority only increased after the interval; Morocco's midfield couldn't sustain possession against relentless French pressure. The decisive second came at 67 minutes following a sustained spell of possession—a swift combination involving Tchouaméni ended with a composed volley finishing from the edge of the box. Though Mazraoui and Aguerd marshaled their backline gamely, they lacked the creative spark to trouble Maignan meaningfully. France's performance was controlled rather than spectacular, but utterly professional.

## Tactical Breakdown

France deployed their characteristic 4-3-3 structure, with Upamecano and Saliba forming an imposing defensive partnership. Kanté's box-to-box energy proved crucial in breaking up Morocco's rhythm, while Tchouaméni anchored deeper play-building responsibilities. The fullbacks—Pavard and Hernández—pushed high to overload the flanks, forcing Morocco's wing-backs into defensive postures. Morocco opted for a 4-2-3-1 setup, positioning Riad and Aguerd as a compact pairing, but this rigid structure left them vulnerable to France's fluid passing sequences. Moukoudi and Belammari were tasked with tracking Mbappé and Griezmann, yet the sheer technical superiority of France's attacking third overwhelmed these assignments. France's second-half adjustment of increased tempo—pressing higher up the pitch—completely suffocated Morocco's attempts at building possession, with their central midfielders unable to escape the stranglehold.

## Key Moments

The 34th-minute opener proved transformative. Rabiot received possession 25 yards from goal and threaded a perfectly weighted pass between Morocco's center-backs, catching them in a moment of hesitation. Mbappé's burst of acceleration gave him a yard on Aguerd, and his finish—low, precise, past Bounou's outstretched hands—epitomized French clinical finishing. However, the true turning point arrived at 52 minutes when a French corner routine saw Pavard's in-swinging delivery barely evade the chaos of the box. While cleared, it heralded an unstoppable momentum shift. Within fifteen minutes, France's sustained possession had drained Morocco's resistance; the 67th-minute second goal felt inevitable. A sleek interplay between three France midfielders culminated in a devastating outside-of-the-boot finish that left Bounou helpless.

## Aftermath

This emphatic victory positions France at the summit of their group with three points from one match—establishing immediate psychological authority. For Morocco, defeat stings particularly given their defensive solidity in prior tournaments; they'll reflect on missed opportunities on the counter and a failure to unsettle France's backline. Coach Regragui must reconvene his squad to identify midfield weaknesses that proved catastrophic. France's Didier Deschamps will be satisfied with the balance struck: dominant without excessive risk-taking. The clean sheet particularly pleased, suggesting defensive chemistry already established. Both nations face critical fixtures ahead; France's statement performance has raised Group F stakes considerably, while Morocco must deliver immediate response against secondary opponents to salvage realistic qualification hopes.
