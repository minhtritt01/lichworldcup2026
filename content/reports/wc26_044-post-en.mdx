# Jordan 1–2 Algeria: Post-Match Analysis

## Match Narrative

Algeria's clinical efficiency proved decisive at Levi's Stadium as they overcame a spirited Jordan side in a Group J encounter defined by contrasting halves. Jordan's disciplined defensive setup frustrated Algeria through much of the first period, absorbing sustained North African pressure while remaining dangerous on transitions. The deadlock broke early in the second half when Amine Gouiri finished a well-worked sequence, energizing the Algerian attack. Jordan equalized unexpectedly through Ahmad Dababneh's opportunistic strike in the 67th minute, briefly suggesting a remarkable upset. However, Algeria's superior depth proved decisive when Riyad Mahrez converted from the penalty spot in the 75th minute, securing three vital points and demonstrating why they remain tournament favorites.

## Tactical Breakdown

Jordan deployed a compact 4-3-3 formation, prioritizing defensive stability and exploiting Musa Al-Taamari's mobility through midfield transitions. Their shape remained impressively organized, forcing Algeria to work patiently through the thirds. Algeria countered with their familiar 4-2-3-1 structure, using Houssem Aouar's technical brilliance to probe weaknesses and directing attacks through wider channels. Ramy Bensebaini's aggressive positioning down the left exposed Jordan's flank vulnerabilities early. Algeria's possession dominance (63%) reflected their technical superiority, though Jordan's tactical discipline limited genuine openings until the second-half deterioration forced increasingly desperate forward ventures. Aouar's influence grew as the match progressed, dictating tempo and initiating danger with precision passing.

## Key Moments

The pivotal 75th-minute penalty epitomized Algeria's ruthlessness when opportunities materialized. After Gouiri's opener appeared to have broken Jordan's resistance, Dababneh's equalizer suggested a genuine upset loomed. Yet Mahrez's composed conversion, following an Iyad Haddad handball in the box, proved decisive. Mahrez's placement—low into the corner beyond Yazeed Abulaila's reach—showcased ice-cold finishing under pressure. This moment effectively ended Jordan's fightback, as Algeria subsequently controlled possession, preventing further leveling opportunities. The penalty represented quality asserting itself at a critical juncture, transforming tension into Algerian relief and confirming their superiority.

## Aftermath

Algeria's victory maintains their perfect Group J record, positioning them strongly for knockout qualification with decisive momentum. The three points provide valuable breathing room despite early tournament intensity. Jordan, despite the loss, showed competitive resilience and Dababneh's equalizer provides encouragement, though progressing requires immediate tactical adjustments and clinical finishing against stronger opponents. Algeria's technical staff will view the performance as validation of squad depth, though maintaining intensity remains paramount in subsequent fixtures. Both managers face critical selection decisions ahead, with Algeria eyeing consistency while Jordan must recalibrate ambitions around their defensive solidity and counter-attacking potential.
