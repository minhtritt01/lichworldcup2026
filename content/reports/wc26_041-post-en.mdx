## Match Narrative

Argentina controlled proceedings from kickoff, establishing midfield dominance through Enzo Fernández's press-resistant passing and De Paul's tireless box-to-box running. Austria offered early resistance, attempting to compress Argentina's space and exploit set-piece opportunities, but the South Americans' technical superiority proved decisive. The breakthrough came in the 34th minute when Otamendi rose unmarked from a corner, powering a header past the diving goalkeeper. This goal shifted momentum decisively. Austria pushed higher in the second half seeking an equalizer, but Argentina's counter-attacking proved lethal—a slick three-pass move down the left flank culminated in Álvarez's low drive across goal in the 67th minute sealing the result. The closing twenty minutes saw Austria desperately seeking a consolation, but Argentina's defensive shape remained resolute, limiting clear-cut chances and adding gloss to a professional performance.

## Tactical Breakdown

Argentina deployed a 4-3-3 formation, leveraging their full-backs' attacking thrust while maintaining compactness through three midfielders. Fernández sat deep, acting as a regista to initiate attacks, while De Paul and Rodríguez provided box-to-box support. This shape proved effective against Austria's 4-2-3-1, which struggled to prevent Argentina's circulation from progressing. Austria's double pivot aimed to suffocate Argentina's midfield, but Fernández's range of passing and mobility consistently found free men. Argentina's adjustment at 1-0 was subtle—pushing the wingers wider to stretch Austria's backline—opening spaces for midfield penetration. Austria's manager attempted to counter with more direct play and increased pressing intensity after halftime, but the defensive reorganization exposed defensive vulnerabilities on transitions. Argentina's ability to retain possession (63% share) and dictate tempo proved defining. The tactical chess match tilted decisively toward the higher-ranked side's experience.

## Key Moments

The 34th-minute opening goal proved the turning point. From Argentina's corner delivery, Austria's marking became unsynchronized in the box, with Otamendi drifting into space at the far post unmarked. His powerful header gave the goalkeeper no chance, and visibly lifted Argentina's confidence while deflating Austria's belief. The decisive second goal in the 67th minute demonstrated Argentina's counter-attacking prowess. After Austria lost possession in midfield, a three-man Argentina attack broke forward swiftly. Álvarez received the ball on the left flank, cut inside, and curled a low drive into the far corner—clinical finishing rewarding patient build-up play. These two moments—one from set-play execution, the other from transition speed—encapsulated Argentina's superiority. Austria never recovered mentally after the second goal, with the remaining half-hour becoming academic despite occasional promising moments.
