# Ivory Coast Dominates Curaçao in Group E Thriller

## Match Narrative

Ivory Coast delivered a clinical performance at Lincoln Financial Field, controlling the contest from kickoff and securing a commanding 2–0 victory over a spirited but outmatched Curaçao. The Caribbean side entered defensively, content to sit deep in a 5-4-1 shape, but Ivory Coast's rhythmic passing soon found its groove. The opening 25 minutes saw the Elephants probe patiently, probing the edges of Curaçao's compact block. The breakthrough arrived in the 34th minute when Ange-Yoan Bonny capitalized on a loose touch in the Curaçao box, finishing clinically after Seri's through ball. Ivory Coast's second came late in the second half—a counterattacking gem finished by Simon Adingra in the 78th minute after Curaçao pushed numbers forward searching for a lifeline. The match was ultimately decided by gulf in quality and intensity.

## Tactical Breakdown

Ivory Coast orchestrated the encounter from midfield, deploying a fluid 4-2-3-1 with Jean Michaël Seri and Franck Kessié forming an intelligent double pivot that both recycled possession and screened the defense. Wilfried Singo and Odilon Kossounou provided width from fullback, while Adingra and Bonny harassed a tiring Curaçao backline. Curaçao manager opted for a 5-4-1 formation built on compactness rather than ambition—Juninho Bacuna and Leandro Bacuna anchored midfield while Locadia stayed isolated up front. Ivory Coast's pressing in Curaçao's half gradually suffocated the opposition. Though Curaçao showed glimpses of spirit in transition, particularly through Locadia's occasional runs, they lacked the creativity and intensity to trouble goalkeeper Fofana significantly. By the 60th mark, Ivory Coast's control was absolute.
