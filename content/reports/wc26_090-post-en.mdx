## Match Narrative

Morocco's clinical victory over Canada established the African side as a genuine tournament threat. The match began competitively, with Canada pressing high in the opening twenty minutes to disrupt Morocco's rhythm. However, the North Africans absorbed this early pressure and gradually seized control of possession. The breakthrough arrived in the 34th minute when a lapse in Canada's defensive structure allowed Morocco to create space on the wing—Hakimi's overlapping run culminated in a dangerous cross converted decisively at the far post. The goal deflated Canada's resistance, and Morocco entered halftime with commanding momentum. Two further strikes in the second half, both stemming from quick transitions following Canadian possession losses, put the result beyond doubt. Despite spirited resistance from Jonathan David and Liam Millar, Canada lacked the defensive solidity required to contain Morocco's technical superiority and pace on the break. The final scoreline reflects a clear hierarchy between the sides.

## Tactical Breakdown

Canada deployed a compact 4-3-3, prioritizing defensive stability with Eustáquio operating as a holding midfielder alongside Koné. The intention was to compress space and invite Morocco onto them, but Morocco's patient build-up play systematically dismantled this approach. Bounou's sweeping distribution from deep found full-backs Hakimi and Mazraoui in advanced positions, stretching Canada's narrow shape. By mid-first-half, Canada's fullbacks were pinned back, isolating the midfield. Morocco's 4-2-3-1 shape—with a flexible double pivot providing cover—allowed fluid transitions between defense and attack. Belammari and Salah-Eddine's work rate suffocated Canada's creative outlets. Late adjustments from Canada, bringing on fresher legs, achieved little against Morocco's control. The tactical mismatch centered on Morocco's superiority in possession retention and their ability to exploit Canada's reactive structure on the flanks, where defensive coverage repeatedly broke down.

## Key Moments

The pivotal moment arrived at 34 minutes when Nayef Aguerd's pass intercepted a Canadian clearance, initiating a sequence that exposed gaps in the right flank. Hakimi's bursting run down the touchline created the overload Canada's fullback—Joel Waterman—couldn't manage alone. His precise cross was finished confidently at the near post to make it 1-0. The psychological shift was immediate. At 67 minutes, a turnover in midfield triggered a swift three-pass move ending with Mazraoui's low drive across goal—a second strike that killed any Canadian hopes of a comeback. The third goal, on 78 minutes, sealed proceedings following another transition, this time a simple finish after Canada's backline was caught square. Each goal followed a similar pattern: Canadian possession surrendered in dangerous territory, Morocco's press regaining the ball, and clinical finishing. These three moments defined the match's narrative entirely.
