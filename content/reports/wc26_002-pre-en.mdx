# South Korea vs Czech Republic — Group A Preview

## Tactical Analysis

South Korea typically employs a disciplined 4-2-3-1 formation, relying on defensive stability and counter-attacking transitions. Their strength lies in wing play, with Kim Moon-hwan providing offensive width while maintaining shape. The midfield pairing of Lee Tae-seok and Seol Young-woo offers ball recovery and distribution. Kim Min-jae anchors the back line with physical presence and positioning.

Czech Republic prefers a compact 4-3-3, with Ladislav Krejčí controlling tempo from deep. Their fullbacks are industrious but vulnerable to pace down the flanks. Tomáš Holeš and Robin Hranáč provide central stability. However, their midfield can be overrun against high-intensity pressing.

The tactical battle centers on South Korea exploiting Czech's midfield weakness through quick transitions, while Czech Republic must control possession to neutralize the Asian side's counter-threat. Dominating the central third will prove decisive.
