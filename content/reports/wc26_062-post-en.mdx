# Senegal 5–0 Iraq: Dominant Display in Group I Showcase

## Match Narrative

Senegal unleashed a clinical masterclass at BMO Field, dismantling Iraq with five unanswered goals in a one-sided Group I encounter. The Teranga Lions struck early through Idrissa Gueye's 12th-minute opener, capitalizing on Iraq's tentative start and porous midfield transitions. Kalidou Koulibaly's towering header doubled the lead before the half-hour mark, setting the tone for a complete tactical mismatch. Iraq offered minimal resistance, their defensive shape crumbling under sustained Senegalese pressure. Three second-half goals—two from substitute strikers exploiting tired fullbacks—sealed a performance of such dominance that the final scoreline barely captured Senegal's superiority. The match evolved from contest to exhibition after the 35th minute, with Iraq abandoning structure and hoping to minimize further damage.

## Tactical Breakdown

Senegal deployed a dominant 4-3-3 formation, with Gueye and Nampalys Mendy controlling the midfield and forcing Iraq into defensive suffocation. The Lions pressed aggressively in the opening 20 minutes, forcing errors and quick transitions that Iraq's midfield—built around Amjad Attwan's containment role—couldn't process. Iraq attempted a 5-4-1 setup, sacrificing attacking ambition for defensive cover, but the five-man line proved static and vulnerable to Senegal's fluid wing play. Moussa Niakhaté and Fodé Ballo-Touré pushed high from their fullback positions, creating overloads that Iraq's wingers couldn't track. Senegal's superiority only intensified after the second goal; Iraq's manager made no tactical adjustments, allowing Senegal to operate with increasing freedom. By the second half, Senegal rotated freely, maintaining control while testing different attacking combinations against a visibly demoralized opponent.

## Key Moments

The turning point arrived in the 28th minute when Koulibaly powered home a free-kick following a reckless Saman Goran challenge on the left wing—a 2-0 scoreline that psychologically broke Iraq's resolve. Rather than tightening, Iraq's defense became increasingly desperate, with Rebin Sulaka's positioning errors inviting dangerous counterattacks. At 35 minutes, a sweeping Senegalese move down the right flank saw Ballo-Touré cross for an unmarked finisher to make it 3-0, essentially deciding the contest. Iraq never recovered momentum or belief after this sequence. The goal disparity wasn't reflective of tactical experimentation but of Senegal's clinical efficiency and Iraq's complete breakdown in concentration and defensive discipline during this 10-minute window.

## Aftermath

Senegal's demolition sends a statement through Group I: they remain among the tournament's elite attacking forces. The Lions sit comfortably at the summit with maximum points and a vast goal-difference advantage, likely securing knockout progression. Iraq's campaign faces existential questions following this humiliation; defensively shredded and offering no attacking threat, Nashat Akram's side must regroup immediately or risk early elimination. Senegal's manager will note the dominance with satisfaction but scrutinize sloppy second-half defending—tighten this and they're World Cup contenders. For Iraq, the focus shifts to damage control: find defensive solidity, identify set-piece opportunities, and hope remaining fixtures offer redemptive opportunities against weaker opponents.
