# Ivory Coast vs Ecuador — Pre-Match Analysis

## Tactical Analysis

Ivory Coast is expected to deploy a 4-3-3 formation, leveraging their midfield strength with Franck Kessié anchoring and Jean Michaël Seri providing creative distribution. Their key battle zone will be the wide corridors where Wilfried Singo (right) and Odilon Kossounou (left) will provide attacking thrust. Ecuador likely counters in a compact 4-2-3-1, with Willian Pacho and Félix Torres forming a resilient defensive line. The central midfield duel between Seri and Ecuador's defensive duo will be decisive. Ivory Coast's weakness lies in their sometimes-exposed fullbacks on the counter, while Ecuador struggles to break down organized defenses. The match will hinge on Ivory Coast's ability to control tempo and Ecuador's capacity to catch opponents on transitions through pace and technical fluidity.

## Form & Momentum

Both teams arrive with confidence: Ivory Coast's dominant victory over Burkina Faso showcased their cohesion and attacking efficiency, while Ecuador's win against Guatemala demonstrated resilience and composure. Ivory Coast enters as slight favorites, carrying psychological momentum from a convincing display in their opener. Ecuador's success was built on defensive solidity and clinical finishing, suggesting they've found an effective tactical blueprint. However, Ecuador faces a step up in opposition quality here. Ivory Coast's collective experience in continental competition gives them an edge in composure under pressure. Ecuador's motivational edge comes from proving they belong at this level, which could fuel a spirited performance. The psychological narrative favors Ivory Coast, but Ecuador's underdog mentality may prove dangerous.
