## Match Narrative

Iran came out with intensity and control in the opening minutes, establishing possession and probing New Zealand's compact defense. The breakthrough came in the 23rd minute when a quick passing move involving Jahanbakhsh and Gholizadeh carved open the All Whites' right flank, allowing a low cross to find Ezatolahi who finished clinically to the far post. New Zealand's response came unexpectedly through a set-piece vulnerability. In the 37th minute, a quickly-taken corner found Tommy Smith at the back post for a scrappy finish that stunned the Iranian collective. The second half saw Iran reassert dominance, with Beiranvand making crucial saves while Jahanbakhsh's creativity linked defense and attack. New Zealand's resilience paid off again when a long throw-in routine caught Iran's high line napping—Stamenic capitalizing on uncertainty to level in the 64th minute. Iran's leveler arrived in the 78th minute through a Jahanbakhsh free kick that curled past Marinovic, setting up a tense finale.

## Tactical Breakdown

Iran deployed a 4-2-3-1 formation, using Ezatolahi and Moharrami as defensive anchors while Jahanbakhsh roamed the right flank with license to create. Their pressing was organized and high-intensity early on, forcing turnovers in New Zealand's defensive third. However, they were exposed by set-piece routines and struggled with the All Whites' direct play down the wings. New Zealand, set up in a compact 5-3-2 defensive shape, prioritized structural solidity over possession dominance. Under pressure, they invited Iranian attacks, then looked to exploit spaces via quick transition and aerial bombardment from throw-ins. The tactical turning point came after the second New Zealand goal, when Iran's coach widened their attacking lanes, instructing fullbacks to push higher. This created overloads on the wings but simultaneously exposed transition vulnerabilities. New Zealand's failure to capitalize on these gaps proved costly in the final quarter.

## Key Moments

The decisive moment arrived in the 72nd minute when Jahanbakhsh controlled a loose ball 25 yards from goal following a New Zealand clearance. The Iranian winger, known for his range and precision, looked to feed a teammate rather than force a shot, but New Zealand's defense tightened. The subsequent free kick, awarded when a defender clipped Jahanbakhsh, saw the same player unleash a perfectly-weighted curler that bent around Marinovic's dive in the 78th minute. This converted free kick proved the turning point psychologically; Iran's equalizer vindicated their sustained second-half pressure, while New Zealand's defensive resilience couldn't hold. The All Whites threw bodies forward in search of a winner but lacked the creative quality to penetrate further, leaving both teams to settle for a point that felt slightly undercooked for Iran's territorial dominance throughout the match.
