Their defensive line—anchored by Upamecano and Saliba—is well-organized and physically imposing. Norway traditionally favors a 4-4-2 shape, relying on Sander Berge as a midfield anchor to disrupt France's build-up play. The critical battle zone will be the central midfield: if Norway can press aggressively around Kanté and Tchouaméni, they may force turnovers and transition play. However, France's full-backs (Benjamin Pavard and Théo Hernández) are superior athletes who can overload the wings. Norway's weakness is creative depth in midfield; Martin Ødegaard will be isolated if France maintains possession.
